Paragraph 1 (politics)

In the political landscape of the Republic of Zedonia, Prime Minister Maxia has been facing significant opposition from rebellious factions within his own party, the National Unity Party (NUP). The NUP holds a majority in the Zedonian Parliament, earned during the 2018 general election. However, this internal rebellion has raised doubts about Maxia's ability to steer crucial reforms such as the Healthcare Enhancement Bill and the Climate Change Mitigation Act. This internal struggle reached a critical point last month when Deputy Prime Minister Arlen declined to publicly support Maxia's proposed tax overhaul. The rift was further exposed when Arlen, a veteran member of the NUP, questioned the suitability of tax increase strategies during a confrontational parliamentary debate. Maxia's planned economic initiatives heavily depend on these tax reforms, making it difficult for them to progress without unanimous backing from the NUP. Some political analysts argue that Maxia's political future and Zedonia's economic prospects might delay or even derail if the NUP's internal conflicts continue to escalate.

Claim: Prime Minister Maxia's planned economic initiatives heavily rely on tax reform.

Difficulty: Easy

Explanation: The paragraph indicates that Maxia's economic initiatives depend greatly on his proposed tax overhaul. If the tax reform fails, it may stall the progression of these planned initiatives.

Claim: There's a growing divide within the National Unity Party.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: This claim is more challenging to substantiate as it demands more nuanced reading. However, the paragraph provides the evidence, mentioning the rebellion within the party against Maxia's proposed policies and Deputy Prime Minister Arlen's public denouncement of the tax overhaul plan, representing the dissensions within the NUP.

Claim: The 2018 general election was a close run.

Difficulty: Easy

Explanation: There is no information in the paragraph about the specifics of the 2018 general elections in Zedonia. The fact that NUP holds the majority does not provide any insight into the competitiveness of the election.

Claim: The Healthcare Enhancement Bill is aimed at improving the elderly's healthcare.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: While the paragraph mentions the Healthcare Enhancement Bill, it gives no specific details about the content or the targeted demographic of the bill. Claiming that it aims to improve elderly healthcare is therefore unsupported by the given information.

Paragraph 2 (social issue)

The social issue of homelessness is an alarming and widespread dilemma in both affluent nations and developing countries. In New York City alone, the number of homeless people in shelters was estimated to be over 60,000 individuals in January 2021, not including those sleeping on the streets or in other public places - according to the Coalition for the Homeless. Additionally, the United Nations reports that 100 million people worldwide are homeless. This issue is not only a matter of poverty, it also interweaves with other social problems such as unemployment, substance abuse issues, mental health disorders, and systemic failures in providing affordable and effective healthcare. Despite globally increased wealth, a substantial number of people still find themselves unable to afford basic necessities, including secure, decent housing. These people often rely on public spaces for survival which raises questions about their civil rights, social justice, city planning and the efficacy of welfare policy. This sensitive subject demands comprehensive understanding and strategic intervention with community integration, along with a serious commitment from all sectors of society.

Claims

Claim: Homelessness is a social issue in both affluent nations and developing countries.

Difficulty: Easy

Explanation: The paragraph not only clearly states this as fact, but it also provides supporting evidence through the statistic revealing that New York City, an affluent city, had an estimated number of over 60,000 individuals in shelters, in addition to those not in shelters.

Claim: Substance abuse and mental health disorders are some of the issues intertwined with homelessness.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: This claim is substantiated by the paragraph's assertion that the social issue of homelessness is not simply a matter of poverty, but is also connected with other problems including substance abuse and mental health disorders.

Claim: All homeless people rely on public spaces for survival.

Difficulty: Easy

Explanation: While the paragraph mentions that homeless individuals often rely on public spaces for survival, it does not explicitly state that all homeless people use public spaces as their means of survival. Hence, the generalization cannot be substantiated within the context of the paragraph.

Claim: There's an effective collaboration among all sectors to curtail the issue of homelessness.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: This excerpt suggests that tackling the issue needs involvement from all sectors of society, however, it does not provide any evidence or information supporting that an effective collaboration currently exists among all those sectors in their efforts to address homelessness crisis.

Paragraph 4 (travel destination)

The tranquil vineyards of Burgundy, France, renowned as a much-loved travel destination, can offer a sanctuary for wine aficionados as well as history enthusiasts. In this region, history intertwines with viticulture, evidenced by the Clos de Vougeot, a medieval winery established around the 12th century by Cistercian monks. This verdant valley has over 100 registered wineries, known to produce distinctly flavored Pinot Noir and Chardonnay. Annually, it draws thousands of tourists, contributing significantly to the region's economy. A staple to these visits are tailored wine tours, often hosted by knowledgeable locals who share intriguing narratives intertwined with grape harvests and wine pressing. They lead their guests through labyrinthine wine cellars, granite hillsides, and, sometimes, offer insight into the secrets of the ancient process. Such immersive experiences not only enrich tourists' understanding of their beloved wines but also enable cultural exchange and foster relationships between travelers and locals.

Paragraph 5 (finance)

However, skeptics among Wall Street veterans argue that algorithmic trading might increase market volatility, and question whether the system's success can be sustained over the long term.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: This claim is supported by the argument put forward by the Wall Street veterans mentioned in the paragraph. However, this is harder to deduce because it is not as explicitly stated compared to the claim made by Mr. Davis about the new system’s performance.

Claim: Traditional human trading is now obsolete due to the introduction of AI in algorithmic trading.

Difficulty: Easy

Explanation: The paragraph does mention that Mr. Davis contends that traditional human trading is now obsolete. However, this does not necessarily mean it's a universally accepted fact, hence it's not supported with clear evidence in the paragraph.

Difficulty: Hard

Explanation: This claim is not supported because the paragraph only talks about the trading system's performance during its first month. Concerns raised by skeptics among Wall Street veterans about the long-term success further make this claim unsupported by the information given in the paragraph.
